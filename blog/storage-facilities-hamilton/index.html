5 Storage Facilities in Hamilton, ON (With Pricing)

Jordan J. CaronJuly 20, 2026#storage

Whether you're between leases downtown, downsizing off the Mountain, or clearing space during a student turnover near McMaster, Hamilton has a solid range of storage options spread from the lower city out to Stoney Creek, Ancaster, and Waterdown. This list covers five well-reviewed facilities so you can compare features and locations before you commit.


Storage rates in Hamilton shift regularly and promotions change often, so treat these as starting points and confirm current pricing directly with each facility.


1. Access Storage – Victoria Ave N (391 Victoria Avenue North)


Access Storage is one of the largest storage operators in Canada, and its Victoria Avenue North location puts climate-controlled units within easy reach of the lower city, the north end, and the downtown core. Like most of the big brands, they run a first-four-weeks-free promotion for new move-ins Access Storage.


Climate control matters more than usual in Hamilton — Lake Ontario humidity can be hard on furniture, electronics, and anything paper or fabric — so the temperature-controlled units here are worth the small premium for longer-term storage.


Good for: Lower-city and north-end residents, anyone storing furniture or valuables through a humid Hamilton summer.


2. Green Storage – Stoney Creek & East Hamilton


Green Storage's Hamilton-area facility was named 2024 International Facility of the Year by Modern Storage Media, and it shows — the site offers true climate-controlled units, indoor vehicle parking, elevator access, and premium move-in features Green Storage.


It's one of the more modern, well-equipped options in the area, positioned for residents of Stoney Creek and the east end. If you're storing something temperature-sensitive or want vehicle storage alongside your unit, this is a strong choice.


Good for: Stoney Creek and east-end residents, vehicle storage, anyone storing electronics or furniture long-term.


3. SmartStop Self Storage – Centennial Pkwy N (550 Centennial Parkway North)


SmartStop runs indoor, climate-controlled facilities in Hamilton, including its Centennial Parkway North location in the east end and a second site on Sanford Avenue North SmartStop. Both offer temperature-controlled units to protect valuables from Hamilton's humidity, and select locations add vehicle storage.


The Centennial Parkway location is convenient for anyone coming off the QEW or the Red Hill Valley Parkway, which makes loading and unloading straightforward if you're driving in from out of town.


Good for: East-end and Stoney Creek residents, highway-accessible loading, climate-controlled and vehicle storage.


4. Hamilton Self Storage – Ancaster, Dundas, Waterdown & Upper James


For a locally owned alternative to the national chains, Hamilton Self Storage has been operating for 15+ years with locations in Ancaster, Dundas, Waterdown, and on Upper James Hamilton Self Storage. They offer climate-controlled units and 24/7 access.


Their spread of suburban locations makes them especially convenient if you live up on the Mountain or out in the western communities, where the national brands have thinner coverage.


Good for: Mountain, Ancaster, Dundas, and Waterdown residents, anyone who prefers a local operator with 24/7 access.


5. U-Haul Moving & Storage of Hamilton


U-Haul's Hamilton facility keeps units temperature-controlled year-round and pairs storage with truck and trailer rental, moving supplies, and boxes under one roof U-Haul. If you're planning to handle part of the move yourself, having storage and a rental truck at the same location saves a trip.


It's a practical, no-frills option — not the most premium experience on this list, but hard to beat for convenience on a DIY move into storage.


Good for: DIY movers who also need a truck or supplies, short-term storage between homes.


What Does Storage Cost in Hamilton?


Across Hamilton's storage market, a 10×10 unit averages around $180/month, and many facilities run first-month-free or first-four-weeks-free promotions for new customers Findstoragefast. Smaller 5×5 lockers can start well under $100/month, so it's worth measuring what you actually need before you book — most people over-estimate.


One Hamilton-specific note: climate control carries more weight here than in a lot of Canadian cities. Lake Ontario humidity in summer is tough on wood furniture, upholstery, electronics, and anything paper-based, so for anything beyond a few weeks a temperature-controlled unit is usually worth the small premium.
