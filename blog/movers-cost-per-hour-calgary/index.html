How Much Do Movers Cost Per Hour in Calgary?

Jordan J. CaronMarch 18, 2026#moving

Calgary's rental market has changed a lot in the last few years. More people are moving into the city, more condos are going up, and more residents are downsizing, upsizing, or simply relocating within the city than ever before. All that movement means one thing: a lot of people Googling how much movers actually cost before they commit to anything.

Here's a straightforward answer.


What Movers Charge Per Hour in Calgary


For a standard two-person moving crew in Calgary, expect to pay $100–$160/hour, depending on the company, the time of year, and what's included.


At Small Movers Canada, our Calgary rate is $140/hour for two movers, with a three-hour minimum. That puts your starting cost at $390, which comfortably covers most small jobs: a studio apartment, a single bedroom, a furniture-only move, or a storage run.


We're a moving broker, which means we match clients with vetted local crews rather than operating our own fleet. That keeps overhead low and lets us offer consistent pricing without the markup that comes with running a larger operation.


What Drives the Final Cost Up or Down


The hourly rate is your baseline. A few things will affect where you land:

Location within the city. Calgary is large. A move entirely within the Beltline is fast and straightforward. A move from Kensington to the deep southeast — say, McKenzie Towne or Copperfield — adds meaningful drive time, and that counts toward your hours.


Floor and access. Calgary's condo boom has produced many buildings with slow elevators, strict move-in windows, and limited loading zones. If your building requires elevator booking, plan for that to add time. Ground-floor units and houses with direct access are always faster.


Volume and prep. Two movers can typically clear a well-packed one-bedroom in three to four hours. Add a second bedroom, a disassembled bed frame, or a storage locker to empty out, and you're looking at five or six hours. The more packed and ready you are, the faster — and cheaper — the job goes.


Season. Calgary winters are real. Moves in January and February take longer than moves in July — icy parkades, slow elevators, and cold-weather logistics all add time. Summer is peak season, so availability tightens even if rates stay stable.


Hourly vs. Flat-Rate Pricing


This comes up constantly, so it's worth addressing directly.


Hourly pricing works in your favour on smaller, predictable jobs. If your movers finish your Kensington apartment in two and a half hours, you pay for that time (subject to any minimums). The downside is exposure if the job runs long — though for small moves, that's rarely an issue.


Flat-rate pricing gives you a fixed number going in. The trade-off is that flat rates are built with a buffer — companies price for worst-case scenarios, so clean, efficient jobs often cost more than they would on an hourly basis. You'll also need to provide a detailed inventory upfront, and any significant changes on moving day can still prompt a revised quote.


For most small moves in Calgary — a one-bedroom condo in the Beltline, a studio in Bridgeland, a furniture move in Mission — hourly is usually the smarter choice. The job is predictable enough that you won't get burned on time, and you won't pay for contingencies that don't materialize.


Calgary's Condo Market and Why Small Moves Are Common


A significant share of Calgary's recent growth has concentrated in inner-city neighbourhoods — the Beltline, East Village, Inglewood, Kensington, and Bridgeland among them. These areas are dense, rental-heavy, and see high tenant turnover. The result is a steady stream of small moves: one-bedrooms, studios, partial loads, and furniture-only jobs that don't need a full truck or a crew of four.


If that's you, you don't need to pay full-service moving rates. You need two reliable people, the right-size vehicle, and a fair hourly rate.
