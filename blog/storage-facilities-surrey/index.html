5 Storage Facilities in Surrey, BC (With Pricing)

Jordan J. CaronMarch 17, 2026#storage

If you're in the middle of a move and need somewhere to stash your stuff — even temporarily — Surrey has a solid range of storage options depending on your neighbourhood, budget, and how long you plan to store.


This list covers five well-reviewed facilities across the city with current pricing information so you can make a quick comparison before committing. Prices fluctuate, and promotions change regularly, so treat these as starting points and check directly with each facility for today's rates. Surrey has a solid range of storage options depending on your neighbourhood, budget, and how long you plan to store.


1. Sentinel Storage – Newton (13498 73rd Ave)


Sentinel is one of the most consistently well-reviewed storage brands in Canada, and their Surrey location on 73rd Avenue is a strong option for anyone in Newton or the surrounding area.


They offer a wide range of unit sizes — small lockers starting at around $104/month and larger units at $264/month — along with climate control, drive-up access, and a free moving van for new tenants. Current promotions include first four weeks free and a student special at 50% off. Findstoragefast


Good for: Newton residents, students, anyone who wants a well-managed national brand with a local presence.


2. NationWide Self Storage – South Surrey (2337 King George Blvd)


Located in South Surrey near Highway 99 and 24th Avenue, NationWide's King George location is one of the newer facilities in the city — a four-storey, 80,000 sq. ft. building with modern tech including keyless smartphone access.

Pricing starts at $96/month for small units (15–65 sq. ft.), $205/month for medium units, and $385/month for larger spaces. Findstoragefast


The facility includes climate control, 24-hour access, and 24/7 security monitoring.

Good for: South Surrey and White Rock residents, anyone who wants a high-tech facility with strong amenities.


3. Public Storage – Multiple Surrey Locations


Public Storage operates five locations across Surrey, making it a convenient option regardless of which neighbourhood you're in — Whalley, Guildford, or further south. Unit costs vary by size, location, and amenities Public Storage Canada, so it's worth checking which specific location is closest to you before comparing rates.


Current promotions include 50% off for three months on selected units, and prepayment discounts for longer-term rentals. Reservations are free with no deposit required.


Good for: Anyone who wants a well-known brand with multiple location options across the city.


4. Seaboard Self Storage – Central Surrey


Seaboard is a family-owned operation that positions itself as one of the more affordable options in the Lower Mainland. Unit sizes range from 5×5 to 8×20 Seaboardselfstorage, and reviewers consistently mention clean, well-maintained facilities and competitive pricing for long-term storage.


They offer drive-up access 24/7, and the facility also carries moving and packing supplies on-site — handy if you're mid-move and running low on boxes. Appointment required to sign up; call ahead.


Good for: Budget-conscious renters, central Surrey residents, small business owners who need flexible access.


5. Maple Leaf Self Storage – Cloverdale/Surrey–Langley Border


Located just off Landmark Way near 64th Avenue, this facility is well-suited to residents of Cloverdale and Surrey's eastern neighbourhoods Mapleleafstorage, with easy access from major commuter routes. It's been operating for over 40 years and offers drive-up access and vehicle storage in addition to standard units.


Pricing isn't listed publicly, so you'll need to call or check online for current rates — but reviewers note it tends to be competitive for the area.


Good for: Cloverdale residents, anyone needing vehicle or drive-up storage, long-term renters who want a stable, established operator.


What Does Storage Actually Cost in Surrey?


Across Surrey's 22 storage facilities, a 5×10 unit averages around $182/month, while the popular 10×10 unit averages $177/month — though prices start as low as $75/month at some locations. Findstoragefast


As a rough guide: a 5×5 locker holds a closet's worth of items, a 5×10 fits a studio apartment, and a 10×10 works for a one-bedroom home.
